# 03-knowledge — Processed Knowledge

**Purpose:** Where raw captures become durable knowledge. Notes here are processed, linked, and evergreen.

## Subfolders

| Folder | What goes here |
|---|---|
| `permanent-notes/` | Atomic, evergreen insights — one idea per note, written in your own words |
| `literature-notes/` | Processed source material — what you got from a book, article, video |
| `maps-of-content/` | Index pages (MOCs) — entry points into a topic, linking related notes |
| `frameworks/` | Reusable mental models and decision heuristics |
| `guides/` | Operational references — how-to docs for processes you repeat |
| `patterns/` | Recurring themes detected via weekly/monthly review |

## What doesn't go here

- Raw captures → `00-inbox/`
- Unprocessed source material → `04-resources/`
- Project-specific notes → `02-projects/<project>/`
- Personal data (finance, health logs) → `05-areas/`

## The Zettelkasten principle

Every permanent note should:
1. Have exactly one idea (atomic)
2. Be written in your own words (not copy-paste)
3. Link to at least one other note
4. Have a title that is a complete thought ("Links beat hierarchy" not "Links")

## Naming

- Permanent notes: `concept-in-kebab-case.md`
- Literature notes: `author-title-year.md` or `title-source.md`
- MOCs: `MOC-Topic-Name.md`
- Frameworks: `framework-name.md`
- Guides: `how-to-verb-noun.md`
- Patterns: `pattern-YYYY-MM.md`
